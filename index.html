After-School Programs in NYC are Growing Fast.

Have Programs Increased in an Effort to Beat the Achievement Gap?

Tatiana Pinheiro

DEC 16, 2023

At about 2:15 pm each afternoon, the students at P.S./I.S. 218 Rafael Hernandez Dual Language Magnet School prepare for their second learning session of the day. 450 students, grades K-8 are enrolled in an afterschool program called WHEDCO, a non-profit organization serving multifaceted programs throughout the Bronx.

The program at P.S 218 is designed to enrich students' lives by providing homework help, extra-curricular activities, and high school prep for the senior student body. Some of the program’s activities include baking, basketball, gardening, and more. Many after- school programs have taken a multifaceted approach with their curriculum as an attempt to keep students engaged. According to New York State Network for Youth Success, participation in after-school programming increases learning engagement, while decreasing behavioral issues and increasing low attendance rates.

While the expansion of after-school could trigger an alarm of academic decline, it could very well be a great sign of improvement. Jack Zaccara, executive director of One World United and Virtuous, a non-profit organization that specializes in developing accessible after-school programs around the country believes that program growth could be attributed to multiple factors. I think greater understanding from parents, exposure to character education, additional professional development for staff, a sense of lifelong learning, and an after school “community” can make a difference and maybe that’s what’s happening across NYC” he said.

With over 200 listed postal codes, after school programs across NYC totaled at roughly 1600 during 2018.
